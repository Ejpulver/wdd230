Design Principles Document

Easton Pulver

Repetition

Youtube

youtube.com

I think youtube is a good example of repetition because all of the video previews have a lot of the same formatting and white space between them. This shows a consistency of what the user is going to expect from each of these visuals showing which videos there are.

Visual Hierarchy

The Weather Channel

weather.com

The weather channel website uses visual hierarchy right off the bat in a less obvious way unless you are observant. The first couple of videos on the homepage are bigger than the ones below it, signifying some sort of hierarchy between the upper and the lower videos. There is also a similar visual hierarchy in the entire header bar, signifying that there is nothing that particularly needs to grab the users attention, except for the search bar, which is very important for users to find so that they can search weather in their area.

White Space and Clean Design

Apple

apple.com

I've honestly always admired the design of apple's website. They make it look simple while still getting across the information that they need to show. There is plenty of whitespace and everything just fits.
