There are not too many fans more loyal to their team than BYU football fans. They can be seen cheering at local games, wearing BYU merch, or showing their pride at the infamous "holy war" game, where they play against the University of Utah Utes in a battle proving who is the better team.

Downtown Provo offers many different amenities, including a few bars, food, dessert, and more!

The original Provo Temple of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter Day Saints is a well known landmark in the city of provo. Many people come there to worship God and make promises with him. The grounds are very beautiful to walk on as well, with lots of flowers, grass, and beautiful landscaping.

Not too many cities have more than 1 temple in them, but Provo is one of them! This is the Provo City Center Temple, originally the old Provo tabernacle before it got burned down in a horrible fire. But a restoration project was created to salvage the wreckage and it was turned into another temple which is even more beautiful than the original tabernacle. It is also conveniently located in downtown Provo.

The utah valley wasatch mountains are some of the best known features of Provo. In particular, Y mountain, as it is called, has a big Y on the face of it, which represents the Y in Brigham Young University (BYU). There is a hike you can go on to hike up to the Y as well, which attracts many different people.
